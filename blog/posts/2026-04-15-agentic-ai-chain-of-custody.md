# Chain-of-custody for agentic AI

<p class="post-meta">April 15, 2026 · agentic-ai · security</p>

*This is a placeholder draft — replace it with the real essay when you're ready.*

In every industry that takes high-consequence work seriously — aviation, healthcare, nuclear, clinical labs — the question of *who touched what, when, and with what authority* is settled before the work begins, not after.

Software has historically gotten away with much weaker chains of custody. Audit logs, retroactive forensics, the occasional incident review. Agentic AI is now forcing a reckoning that has been overdue for two decades: when an autonomous system is taking actions on a user's behalf, with delegated credentials, against systems of record, the audit trail *is* the system. There is no "after" to investigate without it.

> The chain-of-custody problem isn't new. What's new is who's holding the chain.
